Visual-Hierarchy

Amazon Web Services

aws.amazon.com

When you see this website, you will definitely see the phrase, "AWS Free Tier" first. This is the biggest phrase in this website. Then you wii see The second biggest phrase, "Gain free, hans-on..." Which is the description about AWS Free Tier, and you also have "Learn more about AWS Free Tier", but since this is not an important part compare to other information, the sentence has blue color which is close to the back ground color. At the last, you have "Create a Free Account" with yellow color. When we see red or yellow color, since those colors are stimulating colors, the will easily grab attention from users. In this case, since Amazon want us to use their services, the most important button is big and yellow and standing out.

White Space and clean design

BYU Idaho

https://www.byui.edu/

You can see that the design of this page is using white spaces. The contents is well-organized. This page only has titles and descriptions, logos, and link. Since each of those factors does not occupy too many spaces, this page is so easy to look.

Fitt's Law

Netflix

https://www.netflix.com/

When you see this page, you ca see the Email address bar is almost the biggest thing and at the right center of the page. Under the button, you see "Get Started". By setting E-mail address bar and get started button on the center, users are likely start the process that Netflix want faster.
